# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Professionals (early to mid-career) preparing for salary negotiations. They use this solo, in the days before a real offer conversation. They're anxious and want to feel ready — not judged. They need a safe space to fail, practice, and build confidence before the real thing.

## Product Purpose

A sparring partner for salary negotiation. Users practice against AI negotiators with voice personas across difficulty levels, then get a scored debrief. Success looks like a user who walks into their real negotiation feeling practiced and confident.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, encouraging, coaching. Like a trusted friend who happens to be great at negotiating — not a drill sergeant, not a corporate tool. Warm enough that failing feels safe. Sharp enough that winning feels earned.

## Anti-references

- Corporate HR software (cold, bureaucratic, form-heavy)
- Generic dark SaaS dashboards (indistinguishable, template-feeling)
- Gamified edtech (badges, streaks, infantilizing UX)

## Design Principles

1. **Failure is the point** — the UI should make it feel safe to stumble. Never make errors feel punishing.
2. **Coach, not judge** — feedback tone and visual language should feel like a debrief, not a report card.
3. **Presence over polish** — the negotiation session should feel immersive and real, not like a chatbot widget.
4. **Clarity under pressure** — users are reading and responding in real time; every screen state must be instantly scannable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Voice input is a primary interaction path — must have a robust text fallback. No seizure-risk animations.
